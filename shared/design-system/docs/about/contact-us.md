## Important notice

Before submitting a design review request, please review the following information to ensure your request is complete and can be assessed efficiently.

### Design review priority model

**Urgent**: used for the most time‑sensitive requests. It signals that a response is needed as soon as possible. The urgent review request will be prioritized in current or the upcoming sprint.

> When submitting an urgent request, please include the reason and the expected completion date to help the team plan and prioritize effectively.

**Important**: used for time-sensitive requests. It indicates a high-priority request. These reviews are prioritized in the design system's planning but do not require immediate interruption.

**Medium**: used for default design system review requests. Requests are handled on a first‑come, first‑served basis through the standard review planning process. Capacity is limited, with typically 4 Medium‑priority reviews planned per sprint, and additional requests scheduled for future sprints.

**Low**: used for default explorative design review requests. These requests are handled when capacity allows and can be rescheduled further without risk.

### How to send an effective design review

**Clarify the scope of review**: clearly mark which parts are in scope and out of scope for this design review. This helps reviewers focus on the relevant areas, avoid unnecessary feedback, and provide more accurate and actionable input.

> You can hold `space + the design review marker component` to make sure to put the marker overlay on top of your design, instead of interfering with your design.

For example:

The full page content is new and needs to be reviewed. You can mark the full page in green.

Part of the page is new and needs to be reviewed. You can mark the part of content in green.

Part of the page content is new and requires review, while other parts are out of scope for this review. Please clearly mark the content in scope for review in green, and the content out of scope in red to help reviewers quickly understand what to focus on.
